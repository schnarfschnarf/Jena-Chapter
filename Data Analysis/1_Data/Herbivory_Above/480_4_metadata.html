General *
* Title Community-level herbivory from dBEF Experiment, year 2022
* Abstract Plant community-level herbivory damage was quantified to investigate how herbivory damage relates to plant diversity in treatments of differing plant- and soil-history. We used two different parameteres to describe herbivory. First, the percentage herbivory (damaged leaf area per total leaf area) and second, consumed biomass (the removed leaf biomass per area soil surface; g dryweight per m²). Community-level herbivory was calclulated as the abundance-weighted mean of plant species-specific herbivory values per plot.

Measurements *
* Sampling design For the herbivory damage assessment, samples of the biomass harvest sorted to species have been used. Biomass was cut 3 cm above ground in all treatments of the deltaBEF experiment and the main experiment within a harvest frame of 20x50cm (2 replicates each). To assess herbivory, we used sample 2 from the deltaBEF plots for treatment D1 and D2 of and sample 2 of the Main Experiment plot for treatment D3.
Sample preparation For each species a maximum of 20 leaves were randomly picked. When a species-sample contained less then 20 leaves, all leaves were used for the measurements. In the sampling periods some plant species could not be sampled, as they were not present in the biomass sample.
Sample analysis For each species sample, the absolute area damaged by invertebrate herbivores (in mm²) was estimated by eye as a total value of the three damage types (chewing damage (1), rasping damage (2) and leaf mining damage (3)). To facilitate estimates damaged areas were compared template card with are range of circles and squares of known size for reference. Next, the leaf area of all leaves per species sample was measured with a leaf area meter.
Equipment LI‐3000C and LI-3100C Area Meter, LI‐COR Biosciences, Lincoln (NE) USA
Data Processing *
Data preparation Estimates of leaf area damaged by herbivores and remaining leaf area measured by the leaf area meter were combined based on the the combination of plotcode and species. Species plotcode combinationes were excluded if the two matching separate measurements could not be identified because of missing data or errous labels. Calculation of herbivory values followed the methodology described in Loranger et al. 2014 and Meyer et al 2017. Percentage herbivory was expressed as proportional damage (proportion of damaged leaf area per total leaf area accumulated over the growing period prior to the harvest) and consumed leaf biomass (in gram dry weight) for every plant species/plot-combination. Percentage herbivory was calculated for the three damage types separately and combined in a total value for every species by dividing the damaged leaf area by the original leaf area. Since the area meter measures only the leftover area, which includes leaf mining and rasping damage but not the chewing damage, the original area of leaves was calculated by adding the area of chewing damage to the measured leaf area. To calculate the proportional percentage herbivory per plot (community value), we summed the species-specific percentage herbivory of all species occuring in a community weighted by their relative abundance based on leaf biomass data (plant biomass multiplied with the ratio of leaf biomass to total aboveground biomass). This ratio is the quotient of the species-specific leaf area ratio (LAR) and the specific leaf area (SLA) obtained from the small monocultures (data collected by Roscher et al. 2002-2009, average values over repeated measurements have been calculated for each species and season). To quantify the consumed leaf biomass , the leaf biomass of each species per m² ground surface was multiplied with the respective proportional herbivory damage resulting in the consumed leaf biomass in gram dry weight per m² ground surface. Summing the consumed biomass of every species per plot gives a value of consumed leaf biomass for every community. Whole plots (communities) were excluded if species with missing herbivory data comprised more than 20% of the measured biomass in the plot. If species plotcode combinations with missing herbivory values compromised less than 20% of the total community biomass, the missing herbivory data was imputed by the mean value of the respective species over all available plots.
