General *
* Title Functional and taxonomic table of ITS2 fungi in dBEF plots 2021
* Abstract Fungal OTU assignments - This dataset contains taxonomic and functional assignment of fungal OTUs which was derived from amplicon sequencing of bulk soil samples from dBEF plots. Exact sequence variants were assigned to putative fungal guilds based on their taxonomic annotation and the FungalTraits database. Soil was sampled in May/June 2021. Methods *
Treatment dBEF (plant history, soil history treatment)
Important notes All three dBEF treatments (D1, D2, D3) were sampled.
Measurements *
* Sampling design Sampling was conducted in May/June 2021, i.e. 19 years after the establishment of the main Jena Experiment and 5 years after the establishment of the dBEF Experiment. Bulk soil was collected from each of the 240 dBEF plots (80 plots with different plant communities x 3 treatments). Per plot, soil of 4 soil cores of 3.5 cm diameter and 5 cm depth have been collected and pooled. The soil was stored at -20 °C until further processing.
Sample preparation The soil was thawed, homogenized by sieving (2 mm mesh size), and larger stones and plant parts were removed before DNA extraction. DNA was extracted from approximately 0.25 - 0.3 g of bulk soil using the Quick-DNA Fecal/Soil Microbe Miniprep Kit (Zymo Research Europe GmbH, Freiburg, Germany). Extracted DNA was stored at -20 °C until further usage.
Sample analysis The ITS2 region was amplified using ITS4 and ITS7:ITS7o primers (White et al 1990). Each sample was amplified using 7.5 µl KAPA HiFi HotStart ReadyMix Polymerase and 0.3 µl of each primer and 1 µl of template in 15 µl reaction. The template contained DNA extract with an adjusted concentration of 10 ng µl-1. The following PCR conditions were used: 3 min at 95°C, followed by 30 cycles of 95°C for 20 s, 56°C for 20 s and 72°C for 20 s, final extension of 72°C for 5 min. Three positive PCR products were pooled. AMPure XP beads (Beckman Coulter) were used to purify 30 μl amplicons and the Illumina index and sequencing adapters were added by PCR using a Nextera XT Illumina Index Kit (Illumina Inc., San Diego, USA). Samples were pooled to equal molarity. Paired-end sequencing of 2 × 300 bp was done using a MiSeq Reagent Kit v3 on an Illumina MiSeq System at Department of Soil Ecology of the Helmholtz-Centre for Environmental Research (UFZ, Halle/Saale, Germany).

Data Processing *
Data preparation The snakemake implementation dadasnake (Weißbecker et al., 2020) of the DADA2 pipeline (Callahan et al., 2016) was used for sequence read processing. Sequences of the primers were trimmed from the raw reads using cutadapt (Martin, 2011). Reads were quality-trimmed with a minimum length of 70 bp, truncation of reads at positions with a PHRED score below 15, and discarding reads with a higher expected error than 2 (Callahan et al., 2016). Exact sequence variants (ASVs) were determined, read pairs with a minimum overlap of 15 bp and a maximum of three mismatches were merged, and filtered for chimeras using DADA2’s ‘consensus’ algorithm. Taxonomic classification against the UNITE v8.2 database was performed using the mothur (Schloss et al., 2009) implementation of the Bayesian classifier, followed by BLASTn against the NCBI ITS sequence database (only in case of missing classification from mothur). Non-fungal ASVs were discarded. ASVs were assigned to putative fungal guilds based on their taxonomic annotation and the FungalTraits database (Põlme et al., 2021).
